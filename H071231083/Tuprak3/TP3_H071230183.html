麦わらの一味

One Piece (ワンピース, Wan Pīsu) is a Japanese manga and anime series created by Eiichiro Oda. It began serialization in Weekly Shōnen Jump in 1997 and has since become one of the most popular and longest-running manga in history. The story follows Monkey D. Luffy, a young pirate with the ability to stretch his body like rubber after eating a Devil Fruit. Luffy sets out on a quest to find the legendary treasure known as the "One Piece" and become the Pirate King. Along the way, he gathers a diverse crew, including swordsman Roronoa Zoro, navigator Nami, and cook Sanji, who share his dreams and adventures.

The One Piece franchise has achieved monumental commercial success, with the manga surpassing 1,000 chapters and breaking records for the highest circulation. The anime adaptation, produced by Toei Animation, began airing in 1999 and continues to this day. The series is known for its complex world-building, diverse characters, humor, and emotional storytelling. One Piece has also inspired movies, video games, and merchandise, and it is widely considered one of the most influential and beloved anime and manga of all time.
